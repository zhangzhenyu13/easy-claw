## Your Reasoning Process (Think Step-by-Step)

### STEP 1: Observation Analysis
Carefully analyze the execution result:
- What was the intended outcome of this step?
- What was the actual result?
- Is this a SUCCESS, PARTIAL SUCCESS, or FAILURE?
- What are the key indicators in the output?

### STEP 2: Root Cause Diagnosis
If the step did not fully succeed, identify WHY:
- Parameter issue? (wrong values, missing required fields, format error)
- Tool/Skill issue? (tool limitation, API error, service unavailable)
- Data issue? (invalid input, missing dependency, corrupted data)
- Logic issue? (wrong approach, incorrect assumptions)
- Transient issue? (timeout, rate-limit, temporary network error)

### STEP 3: Recovery Strategy Evaluation
Evaluate each possible path forward:
- CONTINUE: Can we accept the current result and move on? What is the impact?
- RETRY: What specific parameter changes would fix the issue? Why will this work?
- SKIP: Is this step non-critical? What downstream steps will be affected?
- REPLAN: Is the current approach fundamentally flawed? What alternative exists?
- ABORT: Is recovery truly impossible? What are the consequences?

### STEP 4: Decision with Confidence
Make your final decision with a confidence score (0.0 to 1.0):
- CONTINUE requires confidence >= 0.7 (result is acceptable)
- RETRY requires confidence >= 0.5 (parameter adjustment likely to help)
- SKIP/REPLAN/ABORT: explain impact clearly

## Decision Guidelines

**CONTINUE**: Step produced useful/acceptable output. confidence >= 0.7
**RETRY**: Specific parameter adjustments can fix the issue. Must provide adjusted_params. confidence >= 0.5
**SKIP**: Step failure won't block overall task completion. Explain downstream impact.
**REPLAN**: Current plan/approach is fundamentally flawed. Repeated similar failures suggest strategy change needed.
**ABORT**: Failure is truly unrecoverable (e.g., missing critical data, permission denied permanently). Use sparingly.
